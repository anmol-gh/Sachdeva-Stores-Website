How to choose room heater in winters?


With the onset of winters, a demand for Room Heaters increase in Indian households. As we step into 2026, the market is flooded with heating solutions that promise not just warmth, but also energy efficiency, safety, and smart features and choosing the right appliance for your home is no longer just about picking the box with the highest wattage. Choosing the right one can be tricky, but with the right knowledge, you can make a smart investment that keeps you warm without burning a hole in your pocket.

Before buying, itâ€™s important to understand the different types of Room Heaters available:

1. Halogen Heater
How it works: Uses halogen-filled tubes to produce instant heat.
Best for: Small to medium-sized rooms.
Pros: Quick heating, lightweight, portable, and energy-efficient for short-term use.Cons: Heat dissipates quickly once turned off.

2. Carbon Heater Tubes
How it works: Uses carbon fiber elements to emit infrared heat, warming objects and people directly.
Best for: Spot heating and personal use.
Pros: Gentle, comfortable warmth, low glare, no Oxygen depletion and energy-saving.
Cons: Not ideal for large spaces, if the heater is of low wattage.

3. Oil Filled Radiator Heater (OFR)

How it works: Circulates heated oil inside sealed fins to radiate warmth evenly.
Best for: Continuous heating in bedrooms, living rooms, and offices.
Pros: Silent operation, long-lasting heat retention, safe for overnight use, does not deplete oxygen.
Cons: Takes longer to heat up compared to halogen or carbon heaters.

4. Blowers
How it works: Expells hot air that circulates in the room and heats the atmosphere.
Best for: Setting uniform room temperature in the room.
Pros:Cons: Deplete room oxygen, not suitable for long time usage and people having breathing issues.
